The gated-wallet layer

Everything a modern payment system needs, gated end to end.

Liquid Flow wraps any wallet — on any chain — in a programmable approval layer. You define the rules; the gate enforces them on every cent that moves.

Permissioned transfers

Funds move in or out only when your policy approves — quorum signatures, allowlists, velocity limits and time locks, enforced on-chain.

Real-time settlement

Watch inbound collections and outbound payouts settle live, with an immutable ledger that reconciles against chain state continuously.

One API, every chain

A single integration spans EVM chains and non-EVM networks like Solana and Cosmos. Chain adapters handle the differences; you don't.

Approval workflows

Multi-party sign-off with separation of duties. The person who initiates a payout can never be the one who approves it.

Mandates & recurring

Programmable direct-debit and scheduled disbursements — salary runs, vendor cycles, subscriptions — all flowing through the same gate.

Compliance hooks

Built-in slots for KYC, sanctions screening and travel-rule data, so every gated transfer carries the checks your jurisdiction requires.

How the gate works

Four steps from request to settled.

1

Request

A payment in or out is submitted through the API or dashboard, tagged with a unique idempotency key.

2

Evaluate

The policy engine checks allowlists, limits, quorum and screening — failing closed on any uncertainty.

3

Approve

Required signers approve. No single key or person can release funds alone; the gate enforces the quorum.

4

Settle

The transfer executes on-chain, the ledger records it immutably, and reconciliation confirms the match.

Security model

Built so no single failure can move funds.

Defense in depth, least privilege, and fail-closed by default — the security posture is the product, not a setting.

Threshold key custody

Keys are split via MPC — no single machine or person ever holds a complete signing key.

Policy engine, fail-closed

Every outbound transfer is checked against limits, allowlists and screening. Uncertainty blocks the move.

Immutable audit ledger

Append-only, double-entry records of every state change — who, what, when, from where.

Circuit breakers

Freeze transfers system-wide in one action the moment anomalous activity is detected.
